<h4 style="font-weight:normal;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;page-break-after:avoid;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item
2.06.&nbsp; Material Impairments</font></b></h4>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The <i>Tassimo </i>single-serve hot beverage system is part of the
Company&#146;s strategy to enter the &#147;on-demand&#148; coffee segment, an approximately
$1.5 billion sub-segment of the coffee market that is growing at an estimated
30% per annum. In 2004, the Company launched the <i>Tassimo</i>
single-serve hot beverage system in France. It subsequently expanded into six
more countries &#151; Switzerland, the United Kingdom, Germany, the United States,
Canada and Austria. Based on the latest 12-month data available, <i>Tassimo</i> achieved a 12% retail unit share of on-demand
brewers and a 15% dollar share of retail on-demand coffee in markets where <i>Tassimo</i> is sold. Since its launch, <i>Tassimo</i>
has grown to contribute approximately $100 million in incremental annual
revenue for the Company. Nonetheless, as the Company reported in its SEC Form
10-Q filed November 9, &#147;Revenues from the <i>Tassimo</i> hot
beverage system have lagged the Company&#146;s projections. The Company is in the
process of evaluating its business model relative to this product, which may
result in asset impairment charges.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Through its
evaluation process, the Company confirmed that <i>Tassimo</i>
remains an important part of the Company&#146;s coffee strategy in the growing
on-demand segment. The Company also confirmed consumers&#146; positive reaction to
the <i>Tassimo</i> system and high satisfaction
with <i>Tassimo </i>beverages. Based on its
learnings, the Company has developed a revised business strategy for<i> Tassimo. </i>It envisions a smaller, more targeted business
compared to original expectations.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The Company
determined that, under the new <i>Tassimo </i>business
plan, it was over-invested in <i>Tassimo</i>-related
assets, resulting in a non-cash asset impairment charge of $245 million under
SFAS 144. The Company&#146;s Board approved the charge on January 3, 2007. The
impairment charge primarily relates to lower utilization of existing
manufacturing capacity and will be reflected in the Company&#146;s fourth-quarter
2006 financial results. In addition, the Company anticipates that the
impairment will result in related cash expenditures of approximately $3
million, primarily related to decommissioning of idle production lines.</font></p>
