<p align="center" style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">KRAFT FOODS CONTINUES TO FOCUS ITS BRAND PORTFOLIO </font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">THROUGH SALE OF FRUIT SNACKS</font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="color:black;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" color="black" face="Times New Roman" style="color:windowtext;font-size:10.0pt;">NORTHFIELD, IL, April 12, 2005
&#151; Kraft Foods Inc. (NYSE: KFT), a global leader in branded food and beverages, </font><font size="2" color="black" face="Times New Roman" style="color:windowtext;font-size:10.0pt;">announced
today that is has agreed to sell its fruit snacks operation to the Kellogg
Company, for approximately $30 million. The fruit snacks business generated net
revenues of approximately $80 million in 2004.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;This sale is part of our
key strategy of transforming our portfolio,&#148;</font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;"> said Dave Johnson, Kraft&#146;s President, North America
Commercial.<b><font style="font-weight:bold;"> </font></b>&#147;Through innovations, investments, acquisitions and divestitures, we&#146;re
focusing our portfolio on businesses that offer Kraft sustainable competitive
advantage.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="color:black;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" color="black" face="Times New Roman" style="color:windowtext;font-size:10.0pt;">The sale includes Kraft&#146;s
31<sup>st</sup> Street manufacturing facility in Chicago, IL. As part of the
transaction, virtually all of the 400 employees there will join the Kellogg
Company. &#147;The employees at the fruit snacks facility have done an outstanding
job; we wish them continued success in the future,&#148; said Johnson.</font></p>

<p style="color:black;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" color="black" face="Times New Roman" style="color:windowtext;font-size:10.0pt;">Kraft incurred an asset
impairment charge of $93 million in the first quarter in recognition of the
pending sale of this business. The transaction is expected to close in the
second quarter.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kraft Foods markets many of the world&#146;s leading food
brands, including <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Kraft</font></i> cheese, <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Maxwell House</font></i> and <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Jacobs</font></i> coffees,
<i><font style="font-style:italic;">Nabisco</font></i> cookies and crackers, <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Philadelphia</font></i> cream cheese, <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Oscar Mayer</font></i>
meats, <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Post</font></i> cereals and <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Milka</font></i>
chocolates, in more than 150 countries.</font></p>
