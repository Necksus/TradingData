<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item&nbsp;7.01.&nbsp;&nbsp; Regulation FD Disclosure. </font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On February 20, 2007, Kraft Foods Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;)
provided a presentation at the Consumer Analyst Group of New York 2007
Conference.&#160; As previously announced, the
webcast and slides of the presentation will be archived on the Company&#146;s
website, www.kraft.com.&#160; The Company
issued a press release outlining the Company&#146;s three-year growth strategy, 2007
earning guidance and the Company&#146;s long-term financial outlook on February 20,
2007.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The company&#146;s four
strategies are:</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 6.0pt 36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Rewire the organization for growth</b> &#150;<font color="black" style="color:black;"> This includes instilling a
mindset of candor, courage and action throughout the company; putting operating
decisions in the hands of local-market leaders; and focusing the organization
on those goals that will truly drive business growth and shareholder value.</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt .0001pt 36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Reframe Kraft&#146;s categories</b> &#150; The essence of this strategy is
to leverage the power of the portfolio to expand the company&#146;s share of larger,
higher-growth markets.&#160; For example, by
broadening the company&#146;s frame of reference in light of how consumers eat
cheese, including cheeses for snacking, for dipping, for sandwiches and for
quick meals, the company expects to increase its share of the large and growing
$14 billion cheese market.&#160; More
specifically:</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt .0001pt 48.0pt;text-indent:-12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#150;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font>The processed cheese
slices category is declining 2%, while sandwich cheese is growing at 2%.&#160; </p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt .0001pt 48.0pt;text-indent:-12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#150;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font>Natural chunks are
growing at 4%, but the broader category of snacking cheese is growing at
6%.&#160; </p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt 48.0pt;text-indent:-12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#150;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font>The
premium cheese segment is growing at a healthy double-digit rate. </p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 6.0pt 36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In addition, the company will move beyond meal components to creating
complete meal solutions.&#160; One example is
the company&#146;s new <i>Oscar Mayer Deli-Creations</i>,
which combine a number of leading brands, like <i>Oscar
Mayer </i>Deli-Shaved meats, <i>Kraft</i>
cheese, <i>A.1.</i> steak sauce, and <i>Grey Poupon</i> mustard, to create convenient,
ready-to-heat sandwiches with fresh-baked taste.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 6.0pt 36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mirroring the approach Kraft took with its successful $250 million <i>South Beach Diet</i> line, the company also will expand its
efforts to use its broad portfolio to meet the needs of specific consumer
groups<i></i>for weight management,
health and wellness and other benefits.</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 6.0pt 36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Exploiting Kraft&#146;s sales capabilities</b> &#150; Kraft will use its
scale to combine the executional benefits of direct store delivery with the
economics of warehouse delivery to drive faster growth in North America.&#160; Internationally, the company will expand its
reach to the traditional trade in key developing markets.&#160; The company plans to build profitable scale
by expanding its distribution reach in countries with rapidly growing demand,
as it has done in Russia and Ukraine.&#160;
Going forward, the focus will be on developing markets where Kraft has
sufficient scale, including Brazil and Mexico.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 6.0pt 36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Driving down costs without compromising quality</b> &#150; The fourth
strategy calls for the company to contain overhead costs, while investing in
quality,&#160; R&amp;D, marketing, sales and
other capabilities that drive or support growth.&#160;&#160; The company plans to complete its $3 billion
restructuring program in 2008, with total annualized savings of $1
billion.&#160; Thereafter, Kraft will spend at
a more consistent level each year to provide a steady stream of savings and
more predictable earnings.</p>

the press release is attached hereto as Exhibit 99.1 and incorporated by
reference into this Item&nbsp;7.01.</font></p>

return Kraft to predictable and consistent growth.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font>&#160;&#160;&#160; Rewire

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font>&#160;&#160;&#160; Reframe

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font>&#160;&#160;&#160; Exploit

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font>&#160;&#160;&#160; Drive
down costs without compromising quality</p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The company expects a return to consistent growth in
three stages.&#160; In 2007, Kraft expects to:</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font>&#160;&#160;&#160; Grow its
top line 3% to 4% organically;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font>&#160;&#160;&#160; Invest
all of its growth, as well as restructuring savings, back into the first wave
of trajectory-changing growth initiatives.&#160;
This represents an incremental $300 million to $400 million investment
in quality, marketing, R&amp;D and capability-building.</p>

current repurchase authority.</font></p>

2007, to Altria shareholders of record as of 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time on March
16, 2007 (the &#147;record date&#148;).&#160; The
repurchase program will end in March, 2009.</font></p>

a significant amount of stock over the next two years,&#148; Rosenfeld said.&#160; &#147;And, it will help to support shareholder
returns until we fully achieve our growth targets.&#148;&#160; Rosenfeld announced the program during her
presentation today at the Consumer Analyst Group of New York Conference.</font></p>
