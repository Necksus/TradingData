August&nbsp;28, 2007, the Kraft Executive Team was advised that James P. Dollive, Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer was retiring. The effective date of his retirement has not yet been determined, but Mr.&nbsp;Dollive has indicated
his willingness to assist Kraft in an orderly transition of his duties and responsibilities. Mr.&nbsp;Dollive&#146;s compensation will continue unchanged during the period prior to his retirement. </FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">On August&nbsp;29, 2007, we issued a press release announcing Mr.&nbsp;Dollive&#146;s retirement and the appointment of Timothy R. McLevish to Executive
Vice President and Chief Financial Officer. A copy of the press release is attached as Exhibit 99.1 to this Current Report on Form 8-K. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B><I>(c)
Appointment of Timothy R. McLevish </I></B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">On August&nbsp;29, 2007, we announced that Timothy R. McLevish, age 52, will become Executive
Vice President and Chief Financial Officer, effective October&nbsp;1, 2007. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish has no family relationships with any of our directors or executive officers. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT
FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Since May 2002, Mr.&nbsp;McLevish has been Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer of Ingersoll-Rand Company Limited, a diversified industrial firm. Prior to joining Ingersoll-Rand, Mr.&nbsp;McLevish was
Vice President, Chief Financial Officer of Mead Corporation, a forest products company, which he joined in 1987 and where he held various positions of increasing responsibility, including president and general manager of Mead&#146;s Specialty Paper
Division. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Information as to whether there are related persons transactions between Kraft and Mr. McLevish is not available at this time.
We will file an amended Form 8-K to disclose any related persons transactions when the information is available. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B><I>(e) Compensation Arrangement with
Mr.&nbsp;McLevish </I></B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Pursuant to our offer letter, (the &#147;Offer Letter&#148;), Mr.&nbsp;McLevish, will be entitled to an annual
base salary of $675,000. He will also be eligible to participate in the Kraft Management Incentive Plan (MIP), which is our annual incentive program, beginning on October&nbsp;1, 2007. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish&#146;s annual target award opportunity under
the MIP is equal to 90% of his salary, or $607,500. The amount he receives will be pro-rated for the period beginning October&nbsp;1, 2007 through December&nbsp;31, 2007 and will also depend on his individual performance and Kraft&#146;s
performance. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish is also eligible to participate in the Long-Term Incentive Plan (LTIP), which is our executive long-term cash incentive program. His target opportunity under the LTIP is equal to 150% of his average base salary during
the three-year performance period. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish&#146;s actual award during the 2007 &#150; 2009 performance period will be pro-rated for the period beginning on October&nbsp;1, 2007 through December&nbsp;31, 2009. The actual amount received
under the 2007 &#150; 2009 LTIP performance period and other subsequent three-year performance cycles will depend on Kraft&#146;s performance during each of the respective performance cycles. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish&#146;s target annual equity award,
which will be equally divided in the form of stock options and restricted stock, is equal to $1,475,000. His equity award is dependent upon his performance for the year. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish will also receive a sign-on cash bonus of $500,000, which he
will be required to repay if he resigns his employment with Kraft within a two-year period. He will also receive a sign-on restricted stock award with a grant value equal to $1,500,000. His restricted stock award will vest one-third each year on the
anniversary of the grant date, over a three-year period. In addition to the standard relocation policy benefits, Mr. McLevish will be eligible for temporary living accommodations for a period of up to seven months. Mr.&nbsp;McLevish is also eligible
for other benefits consistent with those received by our other executives. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The foregoing description of the Offer Letter is qualified in
its entirety by reference to the complete terms and conditions of the Offer Letter, which will be filed with our Form 10-Q for the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2007. </FONT></P>

STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B><U>James Dollive to Retire </U></B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">NORTHFIELD, IL,
August&nbsp;29, 2007 &#151; Kraft Foods Inc. (NYSE:KFT) announced today that Timothy McLevish, 52, will become Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer (CFO), effective October&nbsp;1, 2007. McLevish joins Kraft from Ingersoll-Rand
Company Limited (NYSE:IR), where he has been Senior Vice President and CFO since 2002. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">McLevish succeeds James Dollive, 56, who will be retiring after a
distinguished 29-year career with Kraft. &#147;Jim&#146;s financial expertise and exceptional standards of ethics and integrity have earned him the respect of both his Kraft colleagues and the financial community,&#148; said Irene Rosenfeld, Kraft
Chairman and CEO. &#147;Over the course of Jim&#146;s career, he has helped Kraft achieve many significant milestones, including our initial public offering in 2001 and our recent transition to a fully independent company. We deeply appreciate his
many contributions and wish him the best in his well-earned retirement.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In welcoming McLevish to Kraft, Rosenfeld said, &#147;Tim is an exceptional
executive who combines a distinguished career in finance, accounting and public company governance with experience in line management, strategic planning and mergers and acquisitions. He is a seasoned CFO, with an impressive track record of leading
financial operations for global companies with diversified portfolios. Tim&#146;s extensive experience in driving outstanding shareholder returns and in communicating with investors will be invaluable as we capitalize on our opportunities as an
independent company and restore Kraft to reliable growth.&#148; </FONT></P>
