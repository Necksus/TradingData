Better Focus Its Brand Portfolio</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">NORTHFIELD, Ill., Jan. 23, 2007 &#151; Kraft Foods Inc.
(NYSE:KFT), a global leader in branded food and beverages, announced today that
it has agreed to sell its Hot Cereals business, including the <i>Cream of Wheat </i>brand and related assets to
a wholly-owned subsidiary of B&amp;G Foods, Inc. (AMEX:BGF), for approximately
$200 million. The <i>Cream of Wheat </i>brand
had net revenues of approximately $60 million in 2006.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Our decision to sell <i>Cream
of Wheat </i>is part of a broader effort at Kraft to focus our
portfolio,&#148; said Rick Searer, president, Kraft North America Commercial. &#147;The divestiture
allows us to focus our resources on businesses that offer potential long-term competitive
advantages for us, creating value for Kraft shareholders, while providing B&amp;G
Foods with a great business.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The sale includes the <i>Cream
of Wheat </i>and the <i>Cream of Rice </i>brands
as well as certain manufacturing equipment. In anticipation of the sale, in the
fourth quarter of 2006 Kraft recorded a pre-tax asset impairment charge of $69
million. The sale, which is subject to regulatory approval, is expected to be
dilutive by approximately $(0.01) per share in 2007.</font></p>
