<B><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">KRAFT FOODS STATEMENT REGARDING STARBUCKS AGREEMENT</FONT></B></P>
<P align="left">
<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">NORTHFIELD, Ill. &#150; Nov. 4, 2010 &#150; In response to a statement released today by Starbucks, Kraft Foods Inc. (NYSE: KFT) issued the following clarification regarding the agreement:</FONT></P>
<P align="left">
<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Kraft Foods' agreement with Starbucks regarding the sale of packaged coffee in grocery stores and other channels is perpetual. Importantly, if Starbucks decides to exit its relationship with Kraft Foods, the agreement
requires Starbucks to pay Kraft Foods the fair market value of the business plus, in certain instances, a premium. </FONT><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> </FONT><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Kraft Foods intends to keep the discussions with
Starbucks private and will not be providing further details or comments at this time.</FONT></P>
<P align="left">
<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Kraft Foods is building a global snacks powerhouse and an unrivaled portfolio of brands people love. With annual revenues of approximately &#36;48 billion, the company is the world&#146;s second largest food company,
making delicious products for billions of consumers in approximately 170 countries. The portfolio includes 11 iconic brands with revenues exceeding &#36;1 billion &#150; </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Oreo</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">, </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Nabisco</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> and </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">LU</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> biscuits; </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Milka</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> and </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Cadbury</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> chocolates; </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Trident </FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">gum; </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Jacobs</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> and </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Maxwell House</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> coffees; </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Philadelphia</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face
="sans-serif"> cream cheeses; </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Kraft</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> cheeses, dinners and dressings; and </FONT><I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Oscar Mayer</FONT></I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif"> meats. Approximately 70 brands generate annual revenues of more than &#36;100 million. Kraft Foods (www.kraftfoodscompany.com; NYSE: KFT) is a member of the Dow Jones Industrial
Average, Standard &amp; Poor's 500, Dow Jones Sustainability Index and Ethibel Sustainability Index.</FONT></P>
