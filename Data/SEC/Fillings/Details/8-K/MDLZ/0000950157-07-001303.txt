        shareholders will receive at least 30.3 million shares of Ralcorp stock at
        closing, and Kraft will receive approximately $960 million of cash-equivalent
        value.&#160;&#160;In total, this transaction represents proceeds of
        approximately $2.6 billion to Kraft and its shareholders (based on Ralcorp&#8217;s
        closing stock price of $55.47 on November 14, 2007).</font></div>
      <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; LINE-HEIGHT: 2; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman;">Kraft
        shareholders will receive Ralcorp shares after the distribution and subsequent
        merger of the <em>Post </em>cereals business with Ralcorp.&#160;&#160;Based on
        market conditions prior to closing, Kraft will determine whether the shares
        will
        be distributed in a spin-off or a split-off transaction.&#160;&#160;Either type
        of transaction would be tax-free to Kraft shareholders.&#160;&#160;In a spin-off
        transaction, all Kraft shareholders would receive a <em>pro rata </em>number
        of<em>&#160;</em>shares.&#160;&#160;In a split-off transaction, Kraft
        shareholders would have the option to exchange their Kraft shares and receive
        Ralcorp shares at closing, resulting in a reduction in Kraft&#8217;s shares
        outstanding.</font></div>

        a
        spin-off, Kraft&#8217;s earnings per diluted share would be adjusted downward by $0.13
        on an annual basis.&#160;&#160;From a Kraft shareholder perspective, this is
        largely offset by the earnings attributable to ownership of Ralcorp
        stock.&#160;&#160;In a split-off, Kraft expects earnings dilution on an annual
        basis would be approximately $0.07 per diluted share.</font></div>
