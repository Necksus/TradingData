<p align="right" style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:right;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Exhibit
99.1</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt .0001pt;text-align:center;text-autospace:none;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Kraft Announces New Executive Team;</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;text-autospace:none;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Sharpens
Focus on Innovation, Marketing and Growth</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">NORTHFIELD, Ill. - Sept. 12, 2006 &#151; Kraft Foods Inc.
(NYSE: KFT), a global leader in food and beverages, today announced a new
executive team that will sharpen the company&#146;s focus on innovation, marketing
and growth.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Our new team will build a Kraft that is bolder, more
agile, more creative and more focused,&#148; said <b>Irene
Rosenfeld</b>, Chief Executive Officer of Kraft Foods. &#147;We&#146;re putting
more day-to-day decision-making in the hands of our North American sectors and
international regions &#150; the people closest to our consumers, customers and
markets. And, we will focus more intensely on consumers&#146; evolving food and
beverage needs to take full advantage of our broad product portfolio and to
accelerate growth.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kraft is taking a number of actions to support these
objectives:</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt 36.0pt;text-autospace:none;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Jeri
Finard </b>has been named to the newly created position of EVP &amp;
Chief Marketing Officer to lead Consumer Innovation &amp; Marketing Services.
This new group merges the company&#146;s Global Category Development and Global
Marketing Resources &amp; Initiatives (GMRI) functions. As a result of
combining these two groups, <b>Paula Sneed</b>,
EVP of GMRI, has chosen to leave Kraft after a transition period. During this
time, she will advise the company on a number of marketing excellence
initiatives.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt 36.0pt;text-autospace:none;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Rick Searer </b>has been named EVP &amp;
President of Kraft North America Commercial (KNAC). Under Searer&#146;s leadership,
the Presidents who head the five North American sectors and North America
Foodservice will have significantly more authority to run their respective
businesses. He has been with the company for 25 years, most recently serving as
Group Vice President &amp; President, North America Convenient Meals Sector &#151;
currently the company&#146;s fastest-growing North American sector. Given the
redefined roles within KNAC and a desire to pursue his own career aspirations, <b>Dave Johnson</b>, EVP &amp; President of KNAC,
has chosen to leave the company.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt 36.0pt;text-autospace:none;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font>Some
time ago, <b>Hugh Roberts</b>, EVP &amp;
President of Kraft International Commercial (KIC), informed the company of his
intention to retire. An external search has been under way for his replacement.
Roberts will stay on until early next year to assist with the transition.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt 36.0pt;text-autospace:none;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>David Brearton </b>has been named EVP, Global
Business Services &amp; Strategy, which includes responsibility for areas such
as information systems, business process improvement, and mergers and
acquisitions. <b>Alene Korby </b>will
continue in her role as EVP &amp; Chief Information Officer, reporting to&#160; Brearton, while also assuming leadership for
managing the company&#146;s outsourced and shared services.&#160; <b>Linda
Hefner</b>, EVP, Global Strategy &amp; Business Development, has
expressed a desire to pursue an opportunity outside the company and will leave
Kraft following a transition period.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt 36.0pt;text-autospace:none;text-indent:-18.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b>Marc Firestone </b>has been named to the newly
created position of EVP, Corporate &amp; Legal Affairs and General Counsel. He
will lead the company&#146;s communications, government affairs, community
involvement, law and compliance activities.</p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Continuing in their current roles are: <b>Jim Dollive</b>, EVP &amp; Chief Financial
Officer; <b>Karen May</b>, EVP, Global
Human Resources; <b>Jean Spence</b>, EVP,
Global Technology &amp; Quality; and <b>Franz-Josef
Vogelsang</b>, EVP, Global Supply Chain.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;I want to recognize Dave, Hugh, Paula and Linda for
their exemplary dedication to Kraft,&#148; said Rosenfeld. &#147;Dave&#146;s leadership has
been instrumental in improving the performance of our North American business.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt .0001pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Hugh has built the foundation for the growth we&#146;re
seeing in our developing markets. Paula has been a passionate advocate for
marketing excellence at Kraft. And Linda&#146;s leadership of our M&amp;A activities
has strengthened our portfolio. We wish them much continued success.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The nine members of Kraft&#146;s executive team report to Rosenfeld,
who was named CEO in June 2006.&#160; These
management changes are effective immediately.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Our new management team will tap into the potential
of our people to make the changes necessary to support our quest for faster
growth,&#148; Rosenfeld said.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kraft North America Commercial comprises five consumer
sectors &#151; Beverages, Cheese &amp; Dairy, Convenient Meals, Grocery, and Snacks
&amp; Cereals &#151; and North America Foodservice. Kraft International Commercial
includes four geographic regions: Asia Pacific; Eastern Europe, Middle East
&amp; Africa; European Union; and Latin America.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">About Kraft Foods</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kraft Foods (NYSE: KFT) is the world&#146;s second-largest
food and beverage company. For more than 100 years, Kraft has helped people
around the world eat and live better.&#160;
Millions of times a day, in more than 150 countries, consumers reach for
their favorite Kraft brands including <i>Kraft </i>cheeses
and dinners, <i>Jacobs</i>, <i>Gevalia </i>and <i>Maxwell House </i>coffees, <i>Oscar
Mayer </i>meats, <i>DiGiorno </i>pizzas,
<i>Oreo </i>cookies, <i>Ritz </i>crackers and chips, <i>Philadelphia </i>cream cheese, <i>Milka </i>and <i>Cote
d&#146;Or </i>chocolates, <i>Honey Bunches of
Oats </i>cereals, <i>Good Seasons </i>salad
dressings and <i>Tang </i>refreshment
beverages.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-autospace:none;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">For more information on Kraft Foods,
please visit our website at www.kraft.com.</font></b></p>
