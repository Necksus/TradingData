<p style="line-height:normal;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">NORTHFIELD, IL, January&nbsp;25, 2005 &#151; Kraft Foods
Inc. (NYSE: KFT), a global leader in branded food and
beverages, today announced the election of Richard A. Lerner, M.D., age 66, to
its Board of Directors.&#160; With the
addition of Dr. Lerner, the company&#146;s Board increases from ten to 11 directors,
six of whom are not employees of either Kraft or Altria
Group, Inc.</font></p>

<p style="line-height:normal;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

Lerner is President of The Scripps Research Institute, a private, non-profit
biomedical research organization.&#160;
Previously, he served as Chairman of the Department of Molecular Biology
before becoming president of Scripps, and has held staff appointments at the Wistar Institute in Philadelphia and at the Research
Institute of Scripps Clinic in La Jolla.&#160;
Dr. Lerner is a member of numerous scientific associations, including
the National Academy of Science and the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.&#160; He is also on the Board of Directors for Xencor, a privately held biotechnology company and serves
on the Scientific Advisory Boards of Dyadic, and of Senomyx,
two biotechnology companies.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;I am
delighted to welcome Richard Lerner to our Board of Directors,&#148; said Kraft
Chairman Louis C. Camilleri.</font> &#147;I believe that Kraft will benefit
from both his extensive scientific expertise and his demonstrated business
acumen.&#148;</p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Dr.
Lerner graduated from Northwestern University and Stanford Medical School. He
has received numerous awards and honors from a number of prestigious
institutions, including the </font>Paul
Ehrlich and Ludwig Darmstaedter Prize (2003) for his
achievements in connection with the development of catalytic antibodies.</p>
