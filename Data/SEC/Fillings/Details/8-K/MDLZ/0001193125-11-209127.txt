</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>KRAFT FOODS ANNOUNCES INTENT TO CREATE </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>TWO INDEPENDENT, PUBLICLY TRADED COMPANIES </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><I>Would Create a High-Growth Global Snacks Company and </I></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><I>a High-Margin North
American Grocery Business, </I></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><I>Each with a Portfolio of Iconic Brands </I></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%;padding-bottom:0px;"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">NORTHFIELD, Ill., Aug.&nbsp;4, 2011 &#150; Kraft Foods Inc. (NYSE: KFT) today announced that its Board of Directors
intends to create two independent public companies: A high-growth global snacks business with estimated
revenue</FONT><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="1"><SUP STYLE="vertical-align:baseline; position:relative; bottom:.8ex">1</SUP></FONT><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"> of approximately $32 billion and a high-margin
North American grocery business with estimated revenue of approximately $16 billion. The company expects to create these companies through a tax-free spin-off of the North American grocery business to Kraft Foods shareholders. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;As our second quarter results once again show, our businesses are benefiting from a virtuous cycle of growth and investment, which
we fully expect will continue,&#148; said Chairman and CEO Irene Rosenfeld. &#147;We have built two strong, but distinct, portfolios. Our strategic actions have put us in a position to create two great companies, each with the leadership, resources
and strong market positions to realize their full potential. The next phase of our development recognizes the distinct priorities within our portfolio. The global snacks business has tremendous opportunities for growth as consumer demand for snacks
increases around the world. The North American grocery business has a remarkable set of iconic brands, industry-leading margins, and the clear ability to generate significant cash flow.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Strategic Rationale </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Over the last several years, Kraft Foods has
transformed its portfolio by expanding geographically and by building its presence in the fast-growing snacking category. A series of strategic acquisitions, notably of <I>LU</I> biscuit from Danone and of Cadbury Plc, together with the strong
organic growth of our Power Brands, have made Kraft Foods the world&#146;s leading snacks company. At the same time, the company has continued to invest in product quality, marketing and innovation behind its iconic North American brands, while
implementing a series of cost management initiatives. As a result, the company has delivered strong results in very challenging economic conditions. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="line-height:8px;margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:2px;border-bottom:0.5pt solid #000000;width:10%">&nbsp;</P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Having successfully executed its transformation plan, and 18 months into the Cadbury
integration, the company has, in fact, built a global snacking platform and a North American grocery business that now differ in their future strategic priorities, growth profiles and operational focus. For example, Kraft Foods&#146; snacks business
is focused largely on capitalizing on global consumer snacking trends, building its strength in fast-growing developing markets and in instant consumption channels; the North American grocery business is investing to grow revenue in line with its
categories in traditional grocery channels through product innovation and world-class marketing, while driving superior margins and cash flows. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Over the course of Kraft Foods&#146; strategic transformation, the Board of Directors and management have continually explored opportunities to further enhance performance and increase long-term
shareholder value and believe that creating two independent public companies is the logical next step. Specifically, detailed review by the Board and management has shown that these two businesses would now benefit from being run independently of
each other, rather than as part of the same company. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The company believes that creating two public companies would offer a
number of opportunities: </FONT></P> <P STYLE="font-size:6px;margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px">&nbsp;</P>

</FONT></P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Creating Two Great Companies </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><I></I>Global snacks will consist of the current Kraft Foods Europe and Developing Markets units as well as the North American snacks and confectionery businesses. As an independent company, global snacks
would have estimated revenues of approximately $32 billion and a strong growth profile across numerous fast-growing, attractive markets. Approximately 75 percent of revenues would be from snacks around the world, and approximately 42 percent would
come from developing markets, including a diversified presence in numerous highly attractive emerging markets. The business would have a strong presence in the fast-growing and high-margin instant consumption channel. The non-snacks portion of the
portfolio would consist primarily of powdered beverages and coffee, which have a strong growth and margin profile in developing markets and Europe. Key brands would include <I>Oreo </I>and <I>LU </I>biscuits,<I> Cadbury </I>and <I>Milka
</I>chocolates,<I> Trident </I>gum,<I> Jacobs </I>coffee, and <I>Tang </I>powdered beverages. <I> </I></FONT></P>
 <p STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">2 </FONT></P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The North American grocery business would consist of the current U.S. Beverages, Cheese,
Convenient Meals and Grocery segments and the non-snack categories in Canada and Food Service. With approximately $16 billion in estimated revenue, this business would be one of the largest food and beverage companies in North America. Its portfolio
would include many of the most popular food brands on the continent, with leadership positions in virtually every category in which it competes. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The North American grocery business would have a highly competitive retail presence, cost leadership and a continued commitment to innovation and marketing excellence. North America&#146;s strategic
priorities would be to build on its leading market positions by growing in line with its categories while maintaining a sharp focus on its cost structure. Capitalizing on the investments that the company has made during its transformation, an
independent North American business would be managed to deliver reliable revenue growth; strong margins and free cash flow; and a highly competitive dividend payout. Key brands would include <I>Kraft</I> macaroni and cheese, <I>Oscar Mayer</I>
meats, <I>Philadelphia </I>cream cheese, <I>Maxwell House</I> coffee, <I>Capri Sun </I>beverages, <I>Jell-O</I> desserts and <I>Miracle Whip</I> salad dressing. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Management is targeting capital structures to maintain investment-grade ratings with access to commercial paper for each new entity. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Next Steps </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Management is developing detailed plans for the Board&#146;s
further consideration and final approval. To execute the transaction requires further work on structure, management, governance, and other matters, which will take approximately 12 or more months. The current target is to launch the new companies
before year-end 2012. The company will provide interim updates as appropriate. Throughout the process, management will remain focused on continuing to realize the benefits of the Cadbury integration and delivering strong business results.
</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;Our employees&#146; hard work and accomplishments have transformed Kraft Foods and enabled us to take this next step in
the company&#146;s evolution,&#148; said Rosenfeld. &#147;Our global snacks and North America grocery businesses both have terrifically talented and dynamic people. I&#146;m confident that we will maintain the winning spirit that has been key to our
success.&#148; She continued, &#147;The focus and other benefits that come from creating two independent companies will provide even greater opportunities for our people and our brands.&#148; </FONT></P>
