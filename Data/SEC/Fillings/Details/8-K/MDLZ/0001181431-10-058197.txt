<P align="center">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">KRAFT FOODS CHALLENGES STARBUCKS ATTEMPT TO TERMINATE STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP</FONT></B></P>
<P align="center">
<I><FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Initiates Arbitration Under Agreement</FONT></I></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">NORTHFIELD, Ill. &#150; Nov. 29, 2010 &#150; Kraft Foods Inc. (NYSE: KFT) announced today that it initiated an arbitration proceeding to challenge Starbucks Coffee Company&#146;s attempt
to end the agreement under which Kraft Foods has successfully built Starbucks retail grocery coffee business. </FONT></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">The strategic partnership between Kraft and Starbucks dates back to 1998, when Starbucks retail grocery coffee business was generating less than &#36;50 million in annual revenues. Since
then, Kraft has grown the business to approximately &#36;500 million in annual revenues through its considerable expertise and resources. Over the years, Starbucks has recognized and acknowledged Kraft&#146;s role in building Starbucks presence in
grocery stores.</FONT></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Kraft and Starbucks entered into a contract that remains in effect indefinitely, subject to certain limitations and protections. Notably, the companies agreed to a straightforward basis
under which Starbucks could take over the business in order to pursue a different arrangement. Under the agreement, there needs to be sufficient time for Kraft to execute an orderly transition and Starbucks must compensate Kraft for the fair market
value of the business plus, under most circumstances, a premium of up to 35 percent of that value. </FONT></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">&#147;Starbucks unilaterally and unjustifiably declared in public statements the agreement&#146;s termination, needlessly risking confusion among customers about the agreement&#146;s
status,&#148; said Marc Firestone, Executive Vice President, Corporate and Legal Affairs and General Counsel. &#147;In effect, Starbucks is trying to walk away from a 12-year strategic partnership, from which it has greatly benefited, without
abiding by contractual conditions. Kraft reasonably expected Starbucks to honor the contract. We are confident in our position and look forward to presenting the facts before the arbitrator.&#148; Kraft is continuing to conduct business under the
terms of its contractual arrangements with Starbucks.</FONT></P>
