STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In contrast with the value certainty and upside potential provided by the Offer, Cadbury is asking its shareholders to put their faith in possible future

information on its prospects for 2010. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In this context, Cadbury Shareholders might wish to ask Cadbury the following questions, which are not

markets requires underlying volume growth, how does Cadbury reconcile this requirement with its lack of volume growth to date in 2009? And what is Cadbury&#146;s expectation for volume growth in developed markets in 2010? </FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">2. How will Cadbury deliver its margin targets without further spending on restructuring? </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT

though it has incurred 80% of its targeted costs. How can Cadbury deliver its revised margin targets with only a 25-50 basis point increase in business improvement costs? </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;
