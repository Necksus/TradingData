<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">DEERFIELD, Ill. &#150; Aug. 27, 2013 &#150; Mondelez International announced today that Pradeep Pant, 59, Executive Vice President and President, Asia Pacific and Eastern Europe, Middle
East &amp; Africa (EEMEA), will retire at year-end. Timothy P. Cofer, 44, who currently serves as EVP and President, Europe, will replace Pant. Hubert Weber, 51, President Coffee, Europe, and leader of the company&#146;s Global Coffee Category Team,
will succeed Cofer.</FONT></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">&#147;Pradeep has led the turnaround and dynamic growth of the company&#146;s business in Asia Pacific since 2008,&#148; said Chairman and CEO Irene Rosenfeld. &#147;During his tenure,
Pradeep and his team more than doubled revenue in Asia Pacific through organic growth, while also integrating two large businesses and significantly improving profitability. We sincerely thank Pradeep for his contributions to the business and wish
him well in retirement.&#148;</FONT></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">Rosenfeld continued, &#147;Fortunately, we have a strong bench, and Tim and Hubert are experienced and highly successful leaders. I&#146;m confident they will quickly transition into their
new roles and take these businesses to the next level.&#148;</FONT></P>
<P align="left">
<B><FONT color="#4f2170" face="sans-serif">Cofer: Global Experience Driving Growth with Operating Discipline</FONT></B></P>
<P align="left">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<FONT size=2 face="sans-serif">For more than 20 years, Cofer has successfully delivered strong business results across all of the company&#146;s categories. Since 2011, he has led the company&#146;s business in Europe,
a complex &#36;14 billion region that spans 33 countries. With the benefit of his operating discipline, people development skills and global experience, the European business has outperformed peers, delivering 14 consecutive quarters of growth while
also significantly improving margins, despite the region&#146;s worst recession in decades.</FONT></P>
