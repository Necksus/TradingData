    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">On June
4, 2008, the Compensation and Executive Organization Committee (&#8220;Committee&#8221;) of
our Board of Directors approved a special retention incentive award of 10,000
restricted stock units to Burton H. Snyder, our Senior Vice President, General
Counsel and Secretary, who is one of the executive officers named in the Summary
Compensation Table of our 2008 Proxy Statement.&#160;&#160;The restricted stock
units shall vest in equal proportions on December 31, 2009 and December 31,
2010.</font><br>&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">On June
3, 2008, the Committee also took steps to protect the lump sum retirement
benefit payable to Mr.&#160;Snyder under our Supplemental Executive Retirement
Plan (&#8220;SERP&#8221;).&#160;&#160;Because Mr. Snyder&#8217;s lump sum benefit is subject to
fluctuation due to changing interest rates and other factors affecting
calculation of the lump sum amount under the SERP, his continued employment may
not add to or may erode the amount of benefit payable at
retirement.&#160;&#160;In furtherance of its desire for Mr. Snyder to continue
to serve the Company, and to ensure Mr. Snyder that the lump sum benefit payable
to him under the SERP upon his retirement will not fall below the value
currently projected to be payable to him under the SERP on December 31, 2008,
the Committee agreed to pay Mr. Snyder at retirement an aggregate lump sum
benefit equal to the greater of (i) $5,290,335, or (ii) the amount actually
payable to him under the SERP.&#160;&#160;Information regarding the SERP and Mr.
Snyder&#8217;s accrued retirement benefits are contained in our 2008 Proxy Statement
filed with the SEC on March 10, 2008.</font></div>

    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The
Committee also approved on June 3, 2008 an adjustment in base salary and
incentive target level for John P. Bilbrey, our Senior Vice President, President
Hershey North America, who is an executive officer named in the Summary
Compensation Table in our 2008 Proxy Statement.&#160;&#160;The adjustment
increases Mr. Bilbrey&#8217;s salary from $475,000 to $550,000 and his annual
incentive target to 75% of base salary from 65% of base salary as a result of
updated benchmarking of his position as the North American business he heads
represents approximately 90% of our total revenue.&#160;&#160;In addition, the
Committee made a special retention award of 15,000 restricted stock units to Mr.
Bilbrey which shall vest in equal proportions over the next four
years.</font></div>
