<td align="left" valign="top" width="90%">
<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman">Costs Associated with Exit or Disposal Activities</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">On June 14, 2010,<font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">&#160;</font>the Board of Directors of The Hershey Company (the &#8220;Company&#8221;) unanimously approved project Next Century as part of the Company&#8217;s ongoing efforts to create an advantaged supply chain and competitive cost structure.&#160;&#160;The Next Century capital investment includes a $200 million to $225 million plant expansion of the Company&#8217;s West Hershey facility and approximately $50 million to $75 million in distribution and administrative facilities located in Hershey, Pennsylvania.&#160;&#160;This investment will create a more cost-effective, efficient supply chain that will enable the Company to continue to produce the world&#8217;s best chocolate for years to come.&#160;&#160;The program grew out of the Company&#8217;s previously announced s
upply chain assessment.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">As part of the project, production will transition from the Company&#8217;s century-old facility at 19 East Chocolate Avenue in Hershey, Pennsylvania, to a planned expansion of the West Hershey facility, which was built in 1992. Production from the 19 East Chocolate Avenue plant, as well as a portion of the workforce, will be relocated to the West Hershey facility.&#160;&#160;This change is expected to result in the reduction of approximately 500 to 600 jobs as investments in technology and automation result in enhanced efficiency in the new building. The Company will continue to occupy a significant portion of office space within the historical 19 East Chocolate Avenue facility, including consolidating several local headquarters offices into the vacated space.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The Company estimates that the Next Century program will incur pre-tax charges and non-recurring project implementation costs of $140 million to $170 million over the next three years. This estimate includes charges related to employee separation costs of $55 million to $65&#160;million, plant closure expenses of $45 million to $50 million, net impairment of fixed assets charges of $35 million to $45 million and contract termination costs of $5 million to $10&#160;million.&#160;&#160;The cash portion of the total charge is estimated to be $95 million to $110 million, including project implementation and start-up costs.&#160;&#160;Total capital expenditures related to the program are expected to be $250 million to $300 million.&#160;&#160;At the conclusion of the program in 2014, ongoing annual savings are expected to be approximately $60
