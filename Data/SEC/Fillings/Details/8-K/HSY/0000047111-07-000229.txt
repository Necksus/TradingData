    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman;"><strong>HERSHEY,

      of
      Directors that he intends to retire at the end of 2007. First elected as
      President and Chief Executive Officer of the Company in March 2001, Mr. Lenny
      was elected Chairman of the Board in December 2001.</font></div>
    <div><br></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman;">In

      the Hershey board of directors, stockholders, and employees, we thank Rick
      for
      his leadership over this past six and one-half years. During this time, the
      Company developed and has been executing its value-enhancing strategy with
      the
      over-arching goal of building stockholder value over the long-term. Under Rick&#8217;s
      leadership, through a combination of core brand growth, disciplined global
      expansion, and improved margins, Hershey&#8217;s total stockholder return was higher
      than that of the S&amp;P food group and significantly outperformed the S&amp;P
      500. In addition, Rick has assembled an excellent leadership team that we&#8217;re
      confident will realize Hershey&#8217;s growth potential.</font></div>

      board has begun the succession process and looks forward to making an
      announcement in the near future. Once announced, there will be an orderly
      transition from Rick to his successor. We wish Rick all the best as he concludes
      his role with Hershey,&#8221; Campbell concluded.</font></div>

      challenges, all focused on building a strong foundation for the benefit of
      all
      our stakeholders. I&#8217;m extremely proud of my Hershey colleagues and all that they
      have accomplished. I also want to thank our board of directors for its keen
      insights, support, and guidance over the past several years,&#8221; said Lenny. &#8220;The
      long-term prospects for our category and particularly for our company remain
      promising. I look forward to working closely with my successor<em>&#160;</em>so
